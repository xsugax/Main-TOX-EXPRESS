Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Last updated: March 14, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences, improve functionality, and provide analytics data. Cookies may be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third-party services embedded in the site ("third-party cookies").

2. How We Use Cookies

TOX Express uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for core site functionality such as navigation, session management, and security. These cannot be disabled without impacting site usability.
  • Preference Cookies: Store your settings such as language selection, dark/light mode preference, and regional settings.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website, including pages visited, time spent, and navigation patterns. This data is anonymized and used to improve our services.
  • Performance Cookies: Monitor website performance, load times, and error reporting to ensure a smooth user experience.

3. Cookies We Use

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser. Used for authentication and maintaining your session during tracking lookups and form submissions.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period. Used to remember your theme preference (dark/light mode) and language settings.
  • Google Translate Cookies: Set when using our multi-language translation feature. Governed by Google's privacy policy.
  • Font Service Cookies: Google Fonts may set cookies to optimize font delivery and caching.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services integrated into our website:

  • Google Fonts / Google Translate: For typography and translation services.
  • Pexels: For media content delivery.
  • Font Awesome / cdnjs: For icon and library delivery via CDN.

These services may collect anonymized usage data. We recommend reviewing their respective privacy policies for details.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
  • Set preferences for specific websites
  • Receive notifications when a cookie is set

Please note that disabling essential cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of our website, such as shipment tracking and the customer portal.

6. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or our business practices. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. Continued use of our website after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
